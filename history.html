Suzuki Carry Kei Truck

Kei trucks were introduced in japan in 1959 and are widly used in most of asia because of their small size and cost and recelently some are used in America because of a change in laws they are made to the keijidosha (translated to "light vehicle" and shortened to "Kei") standard of vechiles they are kept a a maximum size of 3.4 m long and 1.48 m wide they are said to have a fuel efficiency of 17 Km/L all japanese models are right wheel drive. Because of some of the differences in safety standards, most American states require them to be at least 25 years or older to be imported and at least 15 years old for Canada. They are often used in the transportation of motorcycles and other small vehicles in the truck bed. They are also used for construction, fishing, and firefighting.
